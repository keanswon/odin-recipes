Description:

Bibimbap is a classic Korean dish that means "mixed rice," typically served with an assortment of sautéed vegetables, sliced beef, a fried egg, and gochujang (Korean chili paste). To make it, start by cooking white rice and preparing toppings like julienned carrots, spinach, bean sprouts, mushrooms, and zucchini—each stir-fried separately with sesame oil and salt. Sear thinly sliced beef (like bulgogi) with garlic and soy sauce. In a bowl, layer the rice, arrange the vegetables and beef on top, and add a sunny-side-up egg. Finish with a generous spoonful of gochujang and mix everything together before eating.
