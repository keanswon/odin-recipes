Taiwanese Popcorn Chicken

Home

Description:

Taiwanese popcorn chicken is a popular street food known for its crispy, bite-sized pieces of marinated chicken, deep-fried with a light, flavorful coating. It's typically seasoned with five-spice powder, garlic, and Thai basil leaves, giving it a bold, aromatic crunch that's both savory and slightly peppery.

Ingredients:

  • 2 lbs beef short ribs (LA-style, flanken cut)
  • 1/2 cup soy sauce
  • 1/4 cup brown sugar
  • 2 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 tbsp mirin (or rice wine)
  • 1 Asian pear (or 1/2 a ripe Bosc pear), grated
  • 1/2 onion, grated
  • 4 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1-inch piece of ginger, minced
  • 1 tbsp sesame seeds
  • 1/2 tsp black pepper
  • 2 green onions, chopped

Instructions:

  1. Rinse short ribs in cold water and pat dry.
  2. In a bowl, combine soy sauce, brown sugar, sesame oil, mirin, grated pear, onion, garlic, ginger, sesame seeds, and black pepper to make the marinade.
  3. Place the ribs in a large resealable bag or dish and pour the marinade over them. Ensure they’re well coated, then refrigerate for at least 4 hours (preferably overnight).
  4. Remove from marinade and grill over medium-high heat for 3–5 minutes per side, until nicely charred and cooked through.
  5. Garnish with green onions and sesame seeds before serving.
